There is a mandatory 20% water reduction. Some homeowners in our neighborhood are over watering their landscape.  Watering should occur between 7pm to 7am.  The excess water is damaging the back side of the mailboxes.  Some homeowners are watering too much that the water runs from the back of their properties, under their driveway and is cracking and lifting the asphalt on the street.  If we have high wind conditions it is pointless to water at all which we encounter on a regular basis.  If it rains, turn the water off for 3 days possibly more, depending on the rain event.  Cool temperatures and fog will allow for a reduction in water usage also.  

Rain sensors and smart controllers are not very expensive these days and there are rebates for controllers and sprinklers on the Socalwatersmart website. There are incentives out there to replace your turf with artifical turf and drought tolerant planting.  

Homeowners will need a pre-inspection before you remove your lawn for a rebate and an approved Architectural Approval from the HOA.
